## Lilinoe Harbottle | BME Graduate Researcher | MedTech Consultant | UCLA Alumna

### Abstract

Building open-source infrastructure for MedTech requires more than just "Open Access" — it requires modular stewardship. This session identifies how architecting for the most rigorous data rights requirements at the community level creates a foundational design that enhances system reliability and data integrity for all users.

Moving beyond standard privacy patches, these facilitated exercises frame the dialogue and prepare participants to identify context gaps in current open-source protocols. Similar to identifying a parallax error in measurement, this session allows participants to experience the friction between universal "Open" assumptions and the relational requirements of community-held data.

Participants will collaborate to explore how relational data architecture creates more robust global health tools, shifting the focus from extractive maintenance to high-integrity stewardship. This session is relevant to the UC ecosystem as it provides a scalable, high-integrity model for researchers and maintainers seeking to bridge the gap between scholarship and production-ready systems.
