We will highlight a commitment to a fully open-source stack, reproducible DevOps practices, and transparent engineering through public repositories. The discussion will emphasize technical DevOps workflows including continuous integration and deployment (CI/CD), infrastructure-as-code, automated testing, containerization, and cost-monitoring dashboards that support sustainable cloud operations. We will also discuss how contributing upstream to core open-source projects strengthens reliability, reduces technical debt, and accelerates innovation across the ecosystem. Drawing on multi-campus implementations such as CAL-ICOR and CloudBank Classroom, developed in close collaboration with 2i2c, we will illustrate how shared infrastructure models enable cross-campus provisioning, environment harmonization, and scalable support for students and researchers. The panel will demonstrate how disciplined open engineering practices can build resilient, extensible computational ecosystems that serve multiple institutions.
