## Abstract

In our workshop, participants will learn how to utilize the Environmental NeTworked Sensor (ENTS) platform to develop wireless sensor networks for environmental and agricultural applications. ENTS is an open source project developed at UC Santa Cruz, which consists of field deployable sensor hardware and web data visualization tools for monitoring uploaded data. ENTS provides a reliable and secure core for handling wireless communication, power management, and data storage. The overhead (monetary, time, maintenance) can be reduced for deploying new sensor applications by writing small applications on utilizing the core components. Recently, ENTS has implemented the open source secure operating system, TockOS, that allows for simplified development of user applications.

ENTS offers a middle ground between commercially available sensing solutions and developing a specialized solution. Commercial solutions such as the ZL6 Datalogger from METER can be costly and suffer from vendor lock-in, while do-it-yourself solutions require significant engineering effort to be made reliable. ENTS has been proven in real-world applications, sensing biological energy sources and corresponding environmental phenomena. Participants will set up the toolchain and develop a demo application for measuring temperature with ENTS. With the gained experience, participants will be able to use ENTS as a platform for building better sensor networks.

## About the Speakers

### John Madden

John is an Electrical and Computer Engineering PhD student at UCSC. His research intersects novel low-power energy sources and wireless sensor networks. He is the maintainer of the Environmental NeTworked Sensor (ENTS) project which he uses to study energy harvesting from soil microbial fuel cells and prickly pear cacti. He is passionate about open-source projects that have real-world use cases in environmental research and agricultural communities. He received his B.S. in Robotics Engineering from the University of California, Santa Cruz in 2020.
