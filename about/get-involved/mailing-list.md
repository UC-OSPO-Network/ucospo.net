A mailing list is one of the most durable ways to stay in touch with your community. We gently encourage folks to join our [Slack](https://join.slack.com/t/uc-ospo-network/shared_invite/zt-3ecp5b20z-ECL~4DkCUslB0t3mbH9xUg) workspace so they can participate fully in our community the benefits of the Network, but maintain mailing lists for those who don't want join us on Slack.

## Platform options

The Network doesn't require a particular platform, and practice varies across campuses. Ask whoever handles email or IT for your unit what's already available before standing up something new. Common options:

- **A campus listserv service:** many UC campuses run a managed listserv (such as Sympa) through central IT—institutionally supported and usually free to you.
- **Google Groups:** quick to stand up if your unit uses Google Workspace, with web archives and self-service subscription.
- **Other tools:** Mailman, a newsletter platform, or whatever your unit already supports and can maintain.

## Bootstrapping your first subscribers

A new list starts empty. A few ways to seed it:

- **Start with people you already know:** colleagues, collaborators, and anyone who's expressed interest.
- **Put a subscribe link on your page:** add a clear call to action on your [web presence](./web-presence.md)
- **Announce it at your first meetup:** bring a sign-up sheet or QR code—the people who show up are your most engaged audience!
- **Ask the Network to cross-promote:** share the list in the [public UC OSPO Network Slack](https://join.slack.com/t/uc-ospo-network/shared_invite/zt-3ecp5b20z-ECL~4DkCUslB0t3mbH9xUg) and we'll help spread the word.
